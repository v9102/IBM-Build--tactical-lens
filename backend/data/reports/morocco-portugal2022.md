# 2022 World Cup Quarter-Final — Morocco 1-0 Portugal

## Formations and tactical shape
Morocco set up in a compact 4-4-2 mid-block that became a 4-5-1 out of possession, with Amrabat sitting deep as the screen. Portugal's 4-3-3 (without Ronaldo from the start) had plenty of possession but lacked penetration. Morocco's discipline was extraordinary — the defensive line held a consistent 35-metre distance from goal, and the midfield four shifted laterally in perfect unison, denying Portugal any passes between the lines.

## Why the goal was enough
En-Nesyri's 42nd-minute header came from a Diogo Costa misjudgement at a cross, but Morocco's plan did not rely on scoring. Their strategy was a zero-risk defensive structure that absorbed pressure. Portugal's 74% possession produced only three shots on target — all from range. Morocco's centre-backs, El Yamiq and Saïss, won every aerial duel, and Hakimi neutralized João Félix's dribbling by staying tight and never diving in.

## The defensive execution
What made Morocco's performance historic was the collective discipline. Every player knew their role: when Portugal shifted right, Boufal tucked into the midfield line; when they shifted left, Ziyech dropped. Amrabat covered 12.5 km, sweeping up counter-attacks before they developed. Portugal's substitutions (Ronaldo, Leão, Silva) changed personnel but not the geometry — Morocco simply reset their block and repeated the same patterns.

## Momentum read
There was no momentum shift in the traditional sense — this was a match where one team refused to let the other seize control. Portugal grew frustrated as the second half wore on, with Ronaldo visibly angry on the bench and later ineffective on the pitch. Morocco's belief grew with every passing 10-minute block. The final whistle confirmed the first African semi-finalist in World Cup history — a triumph of tactical discipline over individual talent.
