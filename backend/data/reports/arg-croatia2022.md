# 2022 World Cup Semi-Final — Argentina 3-0 Croatia

## Formations and tactical shape
Argentina's 4-3-3 with Messi as the free right-winger created constant tactical dilemmas. Croatia's 4-3-3 press tried to trap Argentina in their own half but could not pin down Messi's movement. Croatia's midfield — Modrić, Brozović, Kovačić — was technically superior on paper, but Argentina bypassed it by playing direct to Álvarez's runs and Messi's wide isolations.

## Why the first goal broke Croatia's system
The penalty (34') was won by Álvarez's direct run, not possession football. Croatia's centre-backs, exposed by Argentina's verticality, panicked. Livaković's rush and foul gave Messi the penalty, and the 1-0 lead forced Croatia to commit forward — exactly what Argentina wanted. Croatia had controlled the first 30 minutes but created nothing from it; the goal punished their lack of cutting edge.

## Messi's tactical decapitation
The third goal (70') was the defining moment of Messi's World Cup. Receiving the ball on the right touchline, he isolated Gvardiol — Croatia's best defender — and beat him in a 1v1 over 30 metres. This was not a sprint but a slow burn: Messi dragged Gvardiol out of Croatia's defensive shape, then cut back to the penalty spot where Álvarez had read the play. Gvardiol's desperation to win the ball left the entire right channel exposed.

## Momentum read
Argentina's momentum built in phases: the penalty silenced Croatia's control, the second goal (39', Álvarez's solo run) came before Croatia could regroup, and the third extinguished any hope. Croatia's aging midfield ran out of steam in the second half — Modrić (37), Brozović (30), and Kovačić could not match Argentina's intensity after chasing the game for 45 minutes.
