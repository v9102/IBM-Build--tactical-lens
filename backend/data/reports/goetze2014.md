# 2014 World Cup Final — Germany 1-0 Argentina (a.e.t.)

## Formations and tactical shape
Germany lined up in a fluid 4-2-3-1 that often looked like a 4-3-3 in possession,
with Kroos and Schweinsteiger controlling midfield and the full-backs Lahm and
Höwedes pushing the width. Argentina sat in a compact 4-4-2, conceding the ball
and looking to spring Messi and Higuaín on the counter. Germany dominated possession
but Argentina's two banks of four denied space between the lines for long stretches.

## Why the substitution mattered
With Klose tiring, Joachim Löw introduced Mario Götze in the 88th minute. Götze gave
Germany a more mobile false-nine profile who drifted into the left half-space rather
than holding central like Klose. That movement pulled Demichelis and Garay out of
position and created the pocket Götze later finished from. André Schürrle, already on
for the concussed Kramer, stretched Argentina wide on the left.

## The decisive moment
In the 113th minute Schürrle carried the ball down the left and crossed low-to-medium
height. Götze, attacking the front post zone from the left half-space, killed the ball
on his chest and volleyed across Romero with his left foot. There was no offside or VAR
question — VAR did not exist at the 2014 tournament. It was a clean, well-worked goal
born of fresh legs and movement against a fatigued, deep defence.

## Momentum read
The game's momentum did not swing on a single error but on accumulating fatigue. As
extra time wore on, Argentina's midfield runners (Pérez, Biglia) could no longer track
Germany's rotations, and the substitutes carried fresher legs into the final third.
