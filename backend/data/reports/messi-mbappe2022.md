# 2022 World Cup Final — Argentina 3-3 France (Argentina win 4-2 on penalties)

## Formations and tactical shape
Argentina started in a 4-3-3 / 4-4-2 hybrid built to feed Messi and run in behind via
Álvarez and Di María. For 70 minutes France were passive and overrun in midfield, with
De Paul, Enzo Fernández and Mac Allister winning the second balls. Deschamps' early
double substitution signalled how badly the first hour had gone for the holders.

## The momentum swing
Argentina cruised at 2-0 and seemed in full control. The match turned in 97 seconds:
France won a penalty, Mbappé scored it, and almost immediately he struck a half-volley
to make it 2-2. The shift was psychological as much as tactical — France finally pushed
their forwards higher, and Argentina, who had been managing the game, were suddenly
defending for their lives.

## Key decisions
Three penalties were awarded. Argentina's (23') came from a foul on the onrushing Di
María. France's first (80') was for a trip on Kolo Muani. France's second (118') was a
clear handball by Montiel, with the defender's arm extended away from his body blocking
a goal-bound effort. All three were consistent with the Laws of the Game and the second
France penalty was confirmed without lengthy controversy.

## Momentum read
Extra time became a duel: Messi poked Argentina ahead at 108', Mbappé answered from the
spot at 118'. Argentina held their nerve in the shootout, where goalkeeper Emiliano
Martínez's save and presence proved decisive.
