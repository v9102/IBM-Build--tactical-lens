# 2018 World Cup Round of 16 — France 4-3 Argentina

## Formations and tactical shape
France set up in a counter-attacking 4-3-3 with Kanté and Pogba screening, Matuidi
doing the left-side dirty work, and Mbappé isolated high on the right to attack space.
Argentina, under Sampaoli, fielded an unbalanced shape with a high defensive line and
ageing centre-backs (Otamendi, Rojo) asked to defend large areas. The structural
mismatch was the story: France invited pressure, then released Mbappé behind the line.

## Why the high line failed
Argentina pushed their back four up to compress the pitch and support attacks, but
their defenders lacked the recovery pace to play that high against Mbappé. Every time
France won the ball, a single pass over or through the line turned defence into a foot
race Argentina could not win. This is the classic risk of a high line without quick
centre-backs.

## Key decisions
In the 13th minute Mbappé sprinted roughly 70 metres into the box and was upended by
Rojo. The referee awarded a clear penalty, converted by Griezmann. The decision was
straightforward — a defender catching a runner from behind inside the area — and set
the tone for the day.

## Momentum read
Argentina led 2-1 at one point, but the momentum flipped in a four-minute window
(64'-68') when Mbappé scored twice off transitions. Once France led 3-2, Argentina had
to chase, pushing even more bodies forward and exposing the same space again.
